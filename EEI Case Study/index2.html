Haiti Earthquake Damage

Aerial footage of the earthquake devastation in Port-Au-Prince.

 

 

 

Cathedral of Our Lady of the Assumption, built in 1884 was destroyed in the January 2010 Earthquake killing the archbishop of Haiti instantly. Roman Catholicism is the official religion of Haiti and 97% of Haitians practice catholic or other denominations of Christian worship.

The Roman Catholic cathedral, in the foreground, and the Haiti capital of Port-au-Prince, are in ruins after the 7.0 magnitude earthquake that struck the region on Jan. 12, 2010. (US Navy)

An aerial view of a damaged neighborhood in the Canerpe Vert area of Port-au-Prince. Many poor areas of the capital are built on steep hillsides, creating avalanches of collapsed buildings during the quake. (Christian Al Photography)

Outside of the downtown area of Port au Prince, the hillside next to the Pétion-Ville neighborhood of Morne Lazarre was one of the most devastated areas of the city. Port au Prince, July 10, 2010. (CDN)

Presidential Palace in Port-Au-Prince in ruins after the earthquake.

An arial view of the UN headquarters in Haiti shows the devastation caused by an earthquake measuring 7 plus on the Richter scale rocked Port au Prince Haiti.
